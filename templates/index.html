Introduction

This site explores romance fraud: what it is, who it affects and how the scammers go about it.

What is Romance Fraud?

Romance fraud is an example of a consumer fraud scam. It is similar to your usual scams where the scammer will contact the victim by email, phone, social media etc. In these usual consumer fruad scams the scammer may offer you a get rich quick scheme or contact you with any scenario that warrants a response; often with the goal of scamming you financially. Romance scams differ from this as instead of offering financial motivation, such as getting rich quick, they are offering the victim something more meaningful. Romance fraud scammers are often propositioning their victims with the promise of a relationship, love and a life partner. Romance frauds impacts are much more personal than the usual consumer fraud scam as they can change the victims outlook on life, love and relationships forever.

How Does This Affect Me?

Romance fraud can affect anyone. Romance scammers use everyday social media apps such as instagram and facebook but more commonly dating apps such as tinder, bumble and grindr. Scammers are increasingly harder to spot as they will build realistic profiles usually containing pictures of conventially attractive people with proffesional jobs that would be appealing to most people.

How Common is Romance Fraud?

The number of people falling victim to romance fraud increased by more than 30% last year according to Lloyds Bank. This is due to an increase in the use of dating apps, enhanced by the Covid-19 pandemic.

What Impact Does Romance Fraud Have?

The average loss for a victim of romance fraud last year was £8,234 (a decrease from £8,655 the year before) according to Lloyds Bank. Victims ages 65 to 74 are the most likely to be tricked into sending money to a scammer with the number of victims in this age group increasing by almost 75% last year according to Lloyds Bank and the average amount lost being over £12,000. Romance leaves victims impacted in more ways than finanically. Usually victims are left with emotional trauma. Victims do not want to come to terms with the fact that they have been scammed and that the relationship that may have lasted months or even years was not real.

How Do I Protect Myself?

You can protect yourself by spotting red flags raised by scammers. These can be:

  • Having a lack of information on their profile in dating apps
  • Appearing to be the perfect match for you, too good to be true
  • Having profile pictures that look professionally taken
  • Messaging too much and too often
  • Announcing strong feelings for you very quickly
  • Wanting to move the conversation to a different platform very quickly
  • Coming up with excuses for why they cant show you their face or video / phone call
  • Not having any other social media accounts
  • Requesting any kind of finacial support no matter the story or how small the amount may be
